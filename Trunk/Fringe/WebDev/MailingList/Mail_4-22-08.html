Fringe NEWS (4/22/08)

GAME DEVELOPMENT

By: Justin, Art Director

We have made huge improvements in the game engine! I'm happy to say that we have nearly completed our VIP demo!! The VIP demo is not designed for public release. It is used to help us find the needed revenue to complete The Fringe.

The completion of the VIP demo does mean that we are closing in on the release of our initial installment. I'm pleased to announce that “Team Alpha” now has a new official name: “Miramar Station." This title truly reflects the nature of the first installment. We are working to make Miramar Station much like the Top Gun training course. Development is well underway to create the training facility and the surrounding systems. Look for more information and video in future newsletters.

Vapor trails!! Yeah, I know that in space you may not have vapor trails, but at high speed in nebulous space you very well may! The trails make it easy to see where you are and give you a better feel of distance and position in space… and they look pretty dang cool, too! At the moment, they still need a little work. They do not yet have the look we are going for. Fighters will generate trails on acceleration or hard maneuvers.

We have had quite the call to “Blow Stuff Up!!" lately.

Well, we are several steps closer. Bounding boxes have been created that allow ships to fly close to other ships and to know when they collide. Collision detection allows ships to calculate damage from bullets and missiles. This means objects can be hit and track events such as explosions and bullet hits, and soon we will be able to “BLOW 'EM UP!” WAHAHAHAHA!

KNOW YOUR FIGHTERS

By: Justin, Art Director

Q33-A  LANCE-EAGLE:

 The Q33-A Lance-Eagle is a high velocity, variable wing space, superiority fighter.
It is the 3rd generation fighter in the high performance, low cost Q series engineered by the Colonial Militia; following in the proud tradition of the QX19 Hunter,
and the Q22 Lightening.

Like all other fighters in the series, it uses variable wings and control planes to adjust the center of gravity, thus maximizing flight control and maneuverability.

The Q33-A marks a huge leap in Colonial engineering. The Q33 is the first Colonial fighter to be serviceable in both space and atmospheric flight.
 The hull is smoother and the frame is much lighter than previous designs, allowing for incredible acceleration. The frame is also more rigid and durable, thanks to its multi-point cellular design. Weight has also been reduced by doing away with the gyroscopic cockpit module. There is no room in the cramped Q33 for landing gear, shields, or heavy armor. It is a superior dogfighter and relies on speed and maneuverability to dodge enemy fire.

Onboard firepower is specifically designed to make short work of enemy fighters. The Q33 uses four Vulcan cannons as its primary armament. The usual missile load out is five small warheads housed in an internal magazine. The Q33 can also be outfitted to carry four medium warheads for ship assaults as well.

 The HUD, onboard computer, and gravity dampening systems are all greatly enhanced. This is the first Colonial fighter to allow for the assistance of a COG to do "on the fly" repairs and handle minor ship functions. (However, this feature is limited.)

The Q33 also houses the new line of high thrust engines developed by the Colonials. These engines offer different levels of thrust to improve performance and efficiency. Magnetic thrust vectoring shoots out a wide burst of particles at low speeds to allow for a
 greater "grip" and quicker acceleration. At high speeds, a narrow thrust stream of super high speed particles shoots out to allow for a much higher, top end velocity.

All these improvements aside, the Q33 is still a very short range fighter. It has a small fuel capacity compared with fighters built by the Union and the Federation.  While the engines are more efficient than previous Colonial engines, no fuel saving sacrifices were made that would reduce engine performance.

The Q33 is surprisingly rugged, yet it still does not offer ray shields and has only limited armor plating. Communications systems are fragile and there are no auxiliary systems to backup primary ship systems.

The Lance-Eagle is a dedicated fighter killer, and only pilots who are willing and able to fly by the seat of their pants should attempt to pilot it.
